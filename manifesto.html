Manifesto

For a more humane way to meet & connect — Tribly
They preyed on our attention. Made money from our distraction. Weaponized our desire for connection against us. Made us addicts, staring at our screens. Sucking our souls, stealing our attention— These platforms weren’t built for us, and we weren’t built for them. They claimed to connect us but look how isolated we are. It is time to revolt. It is time for change. It is time for real genuine connection, without addictive design. It is time for social, without the media. It is time to claim our lives back, living it fully, presently, actively. Instead of watching people’s lives, it is time to actually live. Instead of spending countless hours on screens, it is time for us to create meaningful experiences IRL. Instead of watching people do, it is time to do. And this time, not alone— but together. Imagine a world without a loneliness pandemic, where you can find your people anywhere and everywhere. Not just find them, but maintain a healthy connection, regardless where they are. Imagine finding people to paint, workout, dance, code, read, play chess, hike with Spending less time on your phone, and feeling more connected. Love and belonging is an essential need for humans and without this psychological pillar, the hits of life hit stronger and cut deeper. The quality of life is contingent on the quality of our connections. Our mental health. Our ability to solve problems. To experience and share happiness. To grow. Together. We believe that the era for social media is almost over. And we want to be the last nail in its coffin. It’s time to kill social media as we know it. Building something more humane than the attention economy. It’s time for finding real connections, your tribes where you belong. We don’t need another dating app. We don’t need mindless scrolling or psychologically exploiting platforms. We need a tool built to empower humans to find like-minded people and groups. For community builders to strengthen and grow their community. A connection network, that doesn’t make money by gluing us to the screen. To let you out in this world— to find activities, adventures, and connections. We are bringing this imagination to reality. We commit to what “The Social Network” has failed to do… To connect people— genuinely. It’s time for Tribly.

Why this matters

Connection is a human need. We design for presence, not for attention. Healthy communities empower better lives.

Our promise No attention traps

We will build with humane defaults — privacy, discoverability for activities, and tools that move people offline into real life.
